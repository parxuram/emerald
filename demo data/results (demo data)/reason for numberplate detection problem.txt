The OCR results were primarily limited by the source footage rather than the OCR engine itself. Most publicly available traffic videos used during development contained motion blur, compression artifacts, low-resolution vehicles, or intentionally blurred number plates. Even in videos where plates were visible, the cameras were positioned to capture a wide multi-lane scene over a long distance. As a result, individual vehicles occupied only a small portion of the frame, leaving very few pixels for accurate plate recognition after cropping.

Given the hackathon timeline, priority was placed on validating the violation-detection pipeline. OCR was integrated end-to-end, but obtaining ANPR-grade footage with close-range, high-resolution number plates was outside the scope of the prototype. With dedicated plate-focused footage and OCR-specific preprocessing, recognition accuracy would be expected to improve significantly.
